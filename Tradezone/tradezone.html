Project Context

The Analyst Memo

TradeZone recorded impressive headline growth from 2023 to 2024. But beneath the numbers, the data revealed three structural concerns requiring urgent attention before the 2025 planning cycle a conversion gap, a fragile single-engine growth model, and a seller quality imbalance.

This memo was addressed to the Head of Growth and Head of Seller Operations the two teams best positioned to act on the findings immediately.

Summary of Findings

Finding 01 → Head of Growth

Conversion gap: 51–69% of newly acquired customers in 2024 did not make a first purchase within 30 days. The platform is paying to acquire customers it is then failing to activate.

Finding 02 → Head of Growth

Growth engine risk: Q1 2024 delivered 1,544% YoY revenue growth; but every naira came from more orders, not better orders. No repeat purchase behaviour or upselling is visible in the data.

Finding 03 → Head of Seller Operations

Seller imbalance: Only 3 of the top 10 revenue sellers appear on the top 10 speed list. Fast sellers carry ratings as low as 1.85. Speed without quality is commercially worthless on this platform.

Key Findings

Three Problems.
All Fixable.

01

📉 Conversion & Onboarding

Customers Lost at the Door

TradeZone acquired 425 new customers in 2024 but only 177 made a purchase within 30 days, a platform average of 41.65%. Kano performed worst at 31%, Lagos best at 49%. The majority of acquired customers are signing up and going quiet likely due to onboarding friction, poor product discovery, or insufficient trust signals on first visit.

02

⚠️ Revenue Sustainability

1,544% Growth. One Engine.

Orders jumped from 22 in Q1 2023 to 342 in Q1 2024. But average order value stayed flat between ₦250,000–₦360,000. No upselling, no increasing spend, no repeat purchases visible in the data. Every naira of growth came from acquiring more customers meaning if acquisition slows, revenue falls at exactly the same rate with nothing to cushion the impact.

1,544% YoY growth
entirely volume-driven
03

🏪 Seller Operations

Speed ≠ Revenue

Only 3 sellers appear in both the top 10 revenue and top 10 fastest fulfilment lists. Revenue leaders hold ratings of 4.0+. Fast sellers carry ratings as low as 1.85 (GadgetKing NG) and 2.50 (AgriMart NG). Customers tolerate slower delivery from quality sellers and refuse to reward fast-but-poor sellers with repeat purchases or higher spend.

Data Integrity

Data Quality Notes

Every issue encountered was documented transparently — including the decision made and the risk if that decision was wrong. This is non-negotiable in professional analysis.

Issue 01

Missing Product Prices : 4 Products, 97 Order Items

Four products (PROD0104, PROD0088, PROD0205, PROD0245) across Home & Garden, Food & Beverages, and Books & Stationery had no unit_price in the products table; resulting in 97 order items with NULL unit prices and NULL line totals.

Decision Made

Excluded unrecoverable NULL-price order items from all revenue calculations to avoid introducing inaccurate figures.

Risk if Wrong

If these 97 items had significant values, total platform revenue is understated. A data entry audit of these four products is recommended.

Issue 02

Order Amount Mismatches : 9.09% of Orders

Approximately 9.09% of orders showed a difference greater than ₦10 between recorded total_amount and the sum of line items in the order_items table.

Decision Made

Flagged mismatched orders with an amount_mismatch column and retained them using total_amount as the authoritative figure since it represents what was charged to the customer.

Risk if Wrong

If total_amount was itself incorrectly recorded, revenue could be overstated or understated. A reconciliation against the payments table is recommended.

RECOMMENDATION 01

→ Head of Growth

Launch a 30-Day First Purchase Activation Programme

Deploy a structured onboarding sequence targeting all newly registered customers who have not purchased within 7 days of sign-up. Include a time-limited first-purchase incentive (e.g. a ₦2,000 voucher valid 14 days), personalised product recommendations by registration state, and a simplified product discovery experience on first login.

Expected Outcome (60–90 Days)

Increase 30-day conversion from 41.65% to at least 55% representing approximately 42 additional first purchases and ₦10.5 million in incremental revenue at current AOV.

RECOMMENDATION 02

→ Head of Seller Operations

Introduce a Seller Performance Improvement Programme

Identify all active sellers with average ratings below 3.5 and initiate a structured improvement programme mandatory quality audit, coaching on listing accuracy and packaging, and a 90-day performance window to reach 3.8 minimum or face reduced platform visibility. Simultaneously benchmark the top 20 fastest fulfilment sellers and share their practices with high-revenue sellers.

Expected Outcome (60–90 Days)

Elimination of sub-3.0 rated sellers from high-visibility placements. Identification of at least 5 sellers who improve both speed and rating to qualify for a combined performance tier.

Intellectual Honesty

What the Data
Cannot Tell Us

Leadership is likely to ask: which marketing channels are driving the highest-quality customers those who convert fastest and spend the most over time? The current dataset has no acquisition channel data. We know when a customer signed up and where they are, but not whether they came from paid social, organic search, referral, or offline channels.

This means channel-level acquisition cost, conversion rate, and lifetime value are all currently incalculable; limiting how targeted any intervention can be.

Additional Data Requested

A customer_acquisition table linking each customer_id to their source channel, campaign identifier, and acquisition cost. With this, budget can be redirected toward channels producing the highest 30-day conversion rates, making Recommendation 1 significantly more cost-efficient.

Conclusion

Strong Growth.
Fragile Foundation.

TradeZone enters 2025 with genuine commercial momentum ₦984.7 million in revenue, 280 active products, 90 active sellers, and a Q1 2024 that delivered 1,544% year-over-year order growth. These are not small numbers for a multi-city Nigerian platform.

But impressive headline figures can mask structural problems that compound quietly. Three such problems are now visible in the data a conversion gap, a single-engine growth model, and a seller quality imbalance. All three are fixable.

"TradeZone does not have a growth problem. It has a growth quality problem. The platform is acquiring well but activating poorly, growing fast but not deeply. All three recommendations point to the same fix: build depth, not just volume."

- Analyst Conclusion
